Your address is public

If you have a .nz domain name for personal use, chances are good that your name, email address, home address and phone number are all public on the internet.

When you register a .nz domain name, you're asked for your contact details. What may not have been made clear to you is that those details are not kept private between you and the company you're reigstering the domain with. Instead, they are published publicly on the internet for anyone to see as part of the domain registry.

To check whether your details are public, you can use the tool below. If you discover you have accidentally made personal information public there's advice on what to do about it below.

What can I do?

If you have discovered that information you'd rather keep private has been made available, your options are limited. This URL has a good rundown of how to own a .nz domain safely.

Why is this the case?

If it caught you by surprise, you're not the only one. Many of the places that sell .nz domains do not make it very clear that this is occurring. The Domain Name Commission, who run .nz, are currently holding a consultation. If you are concerned about the way your information is being treated, you should send them a quick note at policies@dnc.org.nz noting that this policy has affected you, it will help them make better policy.
